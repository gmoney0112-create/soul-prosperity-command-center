Ad sets per campaign

4. Ad angles & hooks

Five angles to test. Every angle gets at least three hook variants. The winning hook becomes the basis for the next round of variations.

Angle 1

Authority / Mission

You don't need a publisher, a platform, or permission. You need the message and a system. The $7 ebook is the system in 60 minutes of reading.

Angle 2

Story / Testimony

"I wrote the first chapter on a McChicken receipt." Story-led hooks lower friction; the offer rides on the back of belief.

Angle 3

Problem / Pain

"You have a book in you. You also have 47 unfinished Google Docs. Here's why." Speak to the writer who keeps starting and stopping.

Angle 4

Curiosity / Unpopular Opinion

"Most Christian writing advice is wrong. Here's the part nobody says out loud." Pattern-interrupt designed for short-form video.

Angle 5

Direct Offer

"Get the $7 ebook that turns one chapter into a week of writing." Plain and clear — works on retargeting and on warm audiences.

6. Daily budget ramp (qualitative)

The exact dollar amount depends on the operator's ad-account warm-up state and the cost of conversions in the niche. Use this shape, not specific numbers:

  1. Days 1–3 — Calibrate. Run cold-ebook7 at the smallest budget your platform allows and still spend the day's budget. Goal: gather attribution signal.
  2. Days 4–7 — Validate. If at least one ad set is producing purchase_ebook7 events at a CPP that the back-end ladder can carry, hold budget steady. If nothing converts in 3 days at the calibration level, change creative before changing budget.
  3. Days 8–14 — Step up. Increase the spend on profitable ad sets in steps no larger than 20% per 24h. Faster steps trigger learning resets.
  4. Days 15–30 — Scale & layer. Add lookalike ad sets and the retargeting campaign. Refresh creatives weekly.
Spend discipline. Set a weekly ceiling, not a daily one. Budgets shouldn't move more than 20% per 24 hours on a working ad set. The fastest way to kill a winner is to scale it like a loser.

8. Scale & kill rules

TriggerAction
Ad set spent 3× target CPP with zero purchase_ebook7Pause ad set. Replace creative, not audience.
Ad set spent 1.5× target CPP with at least 1 purchase, CPP improvingHold. Re-evaluate at 2× target spend.
Ad set producing CPP < back-end break-even and stable for 72hScale budget +20% per 24h until CPP rises.
CTR collapses by >30% over 3 days at stable spendCreative fatigue — refresh hook/format.
Sales page CVR drops below historical floorStop scaling. Diagnose page first, then ads.
Refund rate > 5% in any 7-day windowPause cold spend. Audit delivery and copy promise.

9. Tracking events & UTMs

Use one UTM convention everywhere. Conversion event names match the static tracking spec so the dashboard, the pixel, and GHL all agree.

UTM template

?utm_source={platform}&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign={campaign}&utm_content={ad_id}&utm_term={angle}

Concrete example:

?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=SP-COLD-EBOOK7&utm_content=reel-mcchicken-v2&utm_term=authority

Required pixel events

  • view_content — sales page loaded
  • begin_checkout — checkout opened
  • add_payment_info — card entered
  • purchase_ebook7 — $7 paid (value 7, currency USD)
  • purchase_audio17 · purchase_paperback27 · purchase_course67 · purchase_lifetime497
  • lead_freebie — opt-in form submit
  • start_skool_trial · start_skool_monthly · start_skool_annual

11. Pre-launch checks

  1. Pixel installed and firing on every page (verified with the platform debugger).
  2. All conversion events listed above firing with a real test transaction.
  3. UTMs landing in GHL custom field freebie_source and on every contact record.
  4. Exclusions configured on all cold ad sets (buyer-course67, refunded, unsubscribed-email).
  5. Retargeting audiences populated with at least one matched user.
  6. Domain verified, advertising-domain compliance set up, business manager admin access confirmed.
  7. Preflight QA walked top to bottom on a real test contact.
